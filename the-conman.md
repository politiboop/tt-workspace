## Chapter 1: The Fraud Before the Presidency

**The Trump Foundation** was not a charity. A New York court found a "shocking pattern of illegality" — charitable donations used to settle business debts, buy a portrait of Trump, and funnel $2.8 million into his 2016 presidential campaign. The court dissolved the foundation and ordered Trump to pay $2 million in damages. It was the first time a sitting president had his charity shut down by a court.

**The Trump Foundation's $25,000 donation to Pam Bondi** is where fraud met politics. In September 2013, four days after Florida Attorney General Bondi's office announced it was "reviewing" the New York lawsuit against Trump University, the Trump Foundation sent a $25,000 check to Bondi's reelection PAC. The donation was illegal — charitable foundations are prohibited from making political contributions. Bondi dropped the review. Her staff retroactively culled 22 consumer complaints down to "one complaint" to justify the decision. The Foundation disguised the donation on its tax returns, listing a Kansas nonprofit instead of Bondi's PAC. Trump paid a $2,500 IRS penalty. CREW filed criminal bribery complaints with the DOJ, IRS, Florida Inspector General, and Florida Ethics Commission.

Then came the rewards: a below-market Mar-a-Lago fundraiser worth roughly $150,000 in value. A presidential endorsement. An RNC speech where Bondi responded to "Lock her up" chants with "I love that." Trump's impeachment defense lawyer. Chair of his think tank's legal arm. That is not a sequence of coincidences. That is a 12-year return on a $25,000 investment.
